Work / JourneyID

Identity Verification

JourneyID

simplified identity verification, mobile telehealth solution

Role

Visual Design + Rapid Prototyping

Timeline

Jan — Sept 2020

Context

Stealth mode startup · Pre-launch

Working with Journey was a fast paced learning opportunity. Prior experience in startup environments provided a reference point for wearing multiple hats and allowed me to create value across app development, marketing and sales.

Early Stage Needs

Journey provided identity verification solutions for digital applications — helping businesses confirm who their users are through document scanning, biometric checks, and real-time validation flows. I was initially brought in on a short-term basis to support specific design needs, but the scope evolved quickly into a broader full-time engagement as the team recognized the range of design challenges ahead of their public launch.

Early work involved supporting a senior designer whose focus was the company website, while I took ownership of product-facing design across the application suite. The nature of a pre-launch startup meant projects shifted constantly — one week deep in UI flows, the next producing sales collateral or working through marketing concepts. That dynamic became a defining feature of the engagement rather than a distraction from it.

Early stage — product and brand design overview

Identity Verification App

Two demo applications were central to Journey's investor storytelling — they needed to work flawlessly and communicate the product's value clearly to non-technical audiences. I took on the task of improving both: updating flows based on accumulated user input, addressing bugs surfaced during internal testing, and tightening the visual presentation to match where the brand was heading.

A sponsored event provided an unexpected source of real-world feedback — putting the demo applications in front of actual users outside the company for the first time and revealing friction points that internal review hadn't caught. Responsive design across screen sizes was an ongoing consideration, particularly for the agent dashboard which needed to function reliably across a wide range of device configurations used by field teams.

Identity verification app — demo flows and investor presentation
Agent dashboard — responsive design across screen sizes

A parallel mobile application extended the core identity verification patterns into a native context, adding features for secure calls and seamless transfers between agents. Designing for mobile introduced constraints around touch targets, session continuity, and the specific trust signals users needed when handing sensitive information through a phone-based interface.

Mobile app — secure calls and seamless agent transfers

Telehealth Iteration

A significant extension of the engagement involved creating a telehealth application that leveraged Journey's existing identity verification infrastructure with an added layer of healthcare-specific functionality. The application needed to support appointment setting, video calling, and payment — each of which introduced its own design requirements while building on the verification patterns already established.

Designing a product at the intersection of identity verification and telehealth meant navigating a high bar for trust and clarity. Users needed to feel confident that their identity was protected while also moving fluidly through appointment and payment flows that had to compete with dedicated, mature healthcare apps.

Telehealth app — appointment, video, and payment flows

Rapid Prototyping

Alongside the product work, I supported the business development team with same-day mockup turnarounds tailored to specific client conversations. These weren't polished final designs — they were fast, credible representations of how Journey's platform could integrate into a prospective client's existing brand and workflow. Mirroring client branding accurately and quickly was the key skill: the goal was for a prospect to see their own product reflected back at them, powered by Journey's infrastructure.

Rapid prototyping — client-branded mockups for business development

Takeaways

This engagement offered a valuable contrast to the more structured design process I had experienced at Flytedesk. The constant shifting of context — from product UI to sales collateral to investor demos — expanded my skillset quickly and built a kind of adaptability that slower-paced environments rarely demand. At the same time, it deepened my appreciation for the benefits of a more methodical approach to design: clear briefs, defined scope, and time to iterate properly. Both modes have their place, and having worked in both made me a more versatile designer.
